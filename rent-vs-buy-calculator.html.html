Frequently Asked Questions

How does the rent vs buy calculator work?

Our calculator compares the total costs of renting versus buying a home over your specified time period. It considers factors like mortgage payments, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, potential investment returns, home appreciation, and tax benefits to provide a comprehensive financial comparison.

What factors should I consider when deciding to rent or buy?

Key factors include: how long you plan to stay in the home, local market conditions, mortgage rates, potential home appreciation, rental prices in your area, your financial stability, and the opportunity cost of using your savings for a down payment versus other investments.

How does home appreciation affect the rent vs buy decision?

Home appreciation can significantly impact the rent vs buy calculation. If home values increase substantially, buying becomes more favorable as you build equity. However, if home values stagnate or decline, renting might be the better financial choice. Our calculator allows you to adjust the expected appreciation rate to see how it affects your decision.

What is the break-even point for buying vs renting?

The break-even point is when the total cost of buying becomes less than the total cost of renting. This varies based on your specific situation but typically occurs after 5-7 years of ownership. Our calculator shows you exactly when this break-even point occurs based on your inputs.
