Financial Goal Planning FAQs

What is a financial goal planner?

A financial goal planner is a tool that helps you define a financial target (like buying a home or saving for college), set a timeline, and calculate the monthly savings needed to achieve it. This AI-powered calculator also factors in your current savings, investment returns, and inflation to give you a precise, actionable plan.

How much should I save for a house down payment in the USA?

A typical down payment for a house in the USA is 20% of the home's price to avoid Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). However, many programs like FHA loans allow as little as 3.5% down. Use this calculator to set your 20% goal, and see our Mortgage Calculator to understand the full costs.

What is a realistic investment return for a 5-year goal?

For a 5-year goal, many advisors suggest a more conservative approach. While the S&P 500 has long-term averages of 8-10%, it's volatile. A blended portfolio might target 4-6%. For short-term goals (under 3 years), a high-yield savings account or CD (providing 3-5%) is often considered safer, as it protects your principal from market risk.

How does inflation affect my savings goal?

Inflation erodes your purchasing power. A $50,000 goal today will require more dollars in the future to buy the same thing. This calculator automatically adjusts your target amount for inflation (using live CPI data as a benchmark) to show you what you'll *really* need to save.
