Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How does the car lease calculator work?

Our AI-powered car lease calculator compares leasing vs buying by analyzing monthly payments, depreciation, interest rates, residual values, and investment returns. It calculates the true cost of both options over time and provides 20+ personalized insights including net worth impact, equity building, tax implications, and financial recommendations based on your specific situation.

Is it better to lease or buy a car?

It depends on your financial situation and lifestyle. Leasing offers lower monthly payments, no maintenance worries (under warranty), and flexibility to drive new cars every 2-3 years. Buying builds equity, has no mileage restrictions, and lets you customize freely. Our calculator analyzes your specific scenario with 20+ AI insights to recommend the best option for maximizing your net worth over time.

What is a money factor in car leasing?

Money factor is the interest rate used in lease calculations, expressed as a small decimal. To convert money factor to APR, multiply by 2400. For example, a money factor of 0.00125 equals 3% APR (0.00125 √ó 2400 = 3%). Lower money factors mean lower monthly lease payments. Always ask dealers for the money factor and convert it to APR to compare with loan rates.

Should I put money down on a car lease?

Generally, no. Financial experts recommend $0 down on leases. If your car is totaled early in the lease, you typically lose any down payment as gap insurance only covers the car's depreciated value, not your upfront payment. It's better to keep that money invested, in an emergency fund, or use it to reduce monthly payments through a higher credit score.

It provides the same calculations dealerships use, plus 20+ AI insights they won't tell you.

What is residual value in car leasing?

Residual value is the estimated worth of the car at the end of your lease term, expressed as a percentage of MSRP. Higher residual values (60-70%) mean lower monthly payments because you're paying for less depreciation. For example, a $30,000 car with 60% residual value means it's worth $18,000 at lease-end, so you only pay for $12,000 of depreciation plus finance charges and tax.

Does the calculator include sales tax?

Yes. The calculator includes sales tax in both lease and buy scenarios. For leasing, you typically pay tax on monthly payments only (varies by state). For buying, you pay tax on the full purchase price upfront (minus trade-in). This can significantly impact the total cost comparison, especially in high-tax states like California (7.25%+) or New York (4%+ state plus local).

Can I compare different down payment scenarios?

Absolutely. You can adjust down payments for both lease and buy scenarios to see real-time impacts on monthly payments, total cost, and net worth. The calculator provides separate input fields for lease down payment and buy down payment, allowing precise comparison of different financing strategies. Try multiple scenarios to find the optimal balance for your budget.

How does the net worth chart work?

The net worth chart visualizes your financial position over time for both options. For buying, it shows equity as you pay down the loan minus depreciation. For leasing, it shows invested savings (the difference in upfront costs and monthly payments) growing with compound returns at your specified investment return rate. This helps you see which option leaves you wealthier at any point in time.
