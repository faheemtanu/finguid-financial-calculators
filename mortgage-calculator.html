Mortgage FAQ

What is a PITI mortgage calculator?

A PITI calculator stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. It's a mortgage calculator that estimates your *total* monthly housing payment, not just the loan payment. This gives you a much more accurate picture of your true homeownership costs.

How is PMI calculated?

PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) is an extra fee, typically 0.5% to 1% of your loan amount annually, required if your down payment is less than 20%. Our calculator automatically adds this to your PITI payment if needed.

What is an amortization schedule?

An amortization schedule is a complete table showing every payment over the life of your loan. It breaks down each payment into how much goes toward principal (paying down your loan) and how much goes toward interest (the cost of borrowing). You can see this in our 'Amortization' tab.

How much house can I afford?

Use our home affordability calculator. As a rule of thumb, many lenders use the 28/36 rule: your housing payment (PITI) should be no more than 28% of your gross monthly income, and all your debt payments no more than 36%.

What is the live FRED rate?

Our calculator uses the FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) API to pull the latest average mortgage rates (like the 30-Year Fixed Rate Mortgage Average, MORTGAGE30US). This provides a real-time, accurate rate for your calculations.

15-year vs. 30-year mortgage: Which is better?

A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but builds equity faster and saves you a significant amount in total interest. A 30-year mortgage has lower, more affordable monthly payments but you will pay much more in interest over the life of the loan. Our AI insights can help you compare these tradeoffs.
