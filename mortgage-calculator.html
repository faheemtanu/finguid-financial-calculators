Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is PITI in a mortgage payment?

PITI stands for Principal, Interest, Taxes, and Insurance. These are the four main components of your total monthly mortgage payment. Principal is the amount that goes toward paying down your loan balance. Interest is the fee you pay to the lender. Taxes refer to the property taxes. Insurance is the homeowner's insurance (and often Private Mortgage Insurance or PMI).

How is Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI) calculated?

This calculator automatically estimates your annual PMI if you have a 'Conventional' loan and your down payment is less than 20% of the home price. It's typically calculated as a percentage of the total loan amount (around 0.5% to 1.5% per year) and is paid monthly until your loan-to-value (LTV) ratio reaches 80% (meaning you have 20% equity).

Where does the 'Live Rate' come from?

Our 'Live Rates' are sourced directly from the Federal Reserve Economic Data (FRED) database. We fetch the latest available weekly average rates for the '30-Year Fixed Rate Mortgage Average' (MORTGAGE30US), '15-Year Fixed Rate' (MORTGAGE15US), and '5/1-Year Adjustable Rate' (MORTGAGE5US1). These are national averages and your actual quoted rate may differ.

How does the ZIP code lookup work?

We maintain a database of average property tax rates and home insurance cost estimates for over 41,000 ZIP codes in the USA. When you enter your ZIP code, the calculator fetches these local averages (as a percentage of home price) and automatically fills in the 'Property Tax' and 'Home Insurance' fields to provide a much more accurate PITI estimate for your specific area.
