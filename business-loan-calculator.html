‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is an SBA 7(a) loan? ‚ñº

An SBA 7(a) loan is the most common type of Small Business Administration loan. It's backed by the SBA (up to 90% guarantee) and can be used for various business purposes including working capital, equipment purchase, real estate acquisition, and debt refinancing. The SBA guarantee allows lenders to offer better rates and terms.

What's the difference between SBA 7(a) and 504 loans? ‚ñº

SBA 7(a) loans are versatile and can be used for multiple business purposes with flexible terms. SBA 504 loans are specifically designed for purchasing real estate or equipment with longer terms (10-25 years) and fixed interest rates. 504 loans require a 10-20% down payment and are better for larger purchases.

What is a good interest rate for a business loan? ‚ñº

Business loan rates vary based on the current prime rate, your credit score, and business financials. As of 2025, typical SBA 7(a) loans range from 7% to 10%, while 504 loans offer fixed rates around 5-6%. Your personal credit score, DSCR, and collateral significantly impact the rate you'll receive.

How is my monthly payment calculated? ‚ñº

Monthly payments are calculated using the standard amortization formula: M = P[r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n-1]. This ensures equal payments over the loan term. The formula factors in the principal amount, monthly interest rate, and number of payments. SBA fees are included in the calculation.

What fees are associated with SBA loans? ‚ñº

SBA 7(a) loans typically include a guarantee fee (1-3% of the guaranteed portion) and a lender packaging fee (around 1%). These fees are usually financed as part of the loan. SBA 504 loans include a CDC fee (1% of the amount financed) and a lender servicing fee.

Can I pay off my business loan early? ‚ñº

Yes, most business loans allow early payoff without prepayment penalties. However, check with your specific lender as some SBA loans may have prepayment penalties if paid off within the first three years for loans with 15+ year terms. Always clarify this with your lender before signing.

What credit score do I need for an SBA loan? ‚ñº

Most SBA lenders prefer a personal credit score of 650 or higher, though some may work with scores as low as 600 if you have compensating factors like strong business performance or collateral. Business credit score is also important. A score above 700 generally qualifies for better rates.

How much can I borrow with an SBA loan? ‚ñº

SBA 7(a) loans can be up to $5.5 million, while SBA 504 loans can be up to $5.5 million for real estate or $5 million for equipment. However, many lenders have their own limits based on your business revenue and creditworthiness. The actual amount you can borrow depends on your business's ability to repay.

What is Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)? ‚ñº

DSCR is your annual net operating income divided by your annual debt service (total loan payments). A DSCR of 1.25 or higher is generally considered healthy by lenders, showing you can comfortably cover loan payments. For example, a DSCR of 1.5 means you earn $1.50 for every $1 of debt service.

How long does it take to get approved for an SBA loan? ‚ñº

Typically 30-90 days from application to funding. The timeline depends on your documentation completeness, business complexity, and lender workload. Some lenders offer expedited processing if all documents are submitted upfront. Having organized financials and business history can speed up the approval process.
