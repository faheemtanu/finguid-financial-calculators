Your Guide to Business Loans in the USA

Understanding business loans is crucial for your entrepreneurial journey. Whether you're a startup seeking seed capital or an established business looking to expand, the right financing can be a game-changer. This calculator helps you decode the numbers, from SBA loans to conventional term loans.

Comparing Loan Types: SBA vs. Conventional

SBA Loans (7(a) and 504): Backed by the U.S. Small Business Administration, these loans typically offer lower interest rates and longer repayment terms. A 7(a) loan is versatile (working capital, equipment), while a 504 loan is for fixed assets (real estate, large equipment).

Conventional Business Loans: Offered by banks and online lenders without a government guarantee. They are often faster to fund but may require stronger credit and a larger down payment. Our calculator helps you compare the monthly cost of each.

What is Cash Flow Analysis?

Our AI-powered insight tool performs a simple but critical cash flow analysis. By entering your estimated revenue and expenses, you can see your **Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)**. Lenders use this metric (Net Operating Income / Total Debt Service) to determine if you can afford your payments. A ratio below 1.25x can be a red flag for many lenders.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What is a good interest rate for a business loan?

As of late 2025, rates can range from 8% to 13% for SBA loans and conventional bank loans. Online lenders may be higher. Our "Get Live Rate" button fetches the current **Bank Prime Loan Rate** from FRED®, a common benchmark used by banks to set their rates.

How do I calculate ROI on a business loan?

To calculate ROI (Return on Investment), you must project the **new profit** generated by the loan. The formula is: `(Net Profit from Loan - Total Loan Interest) / Total Loan Interest`. Our AI insight gives you the "Total Interest" figure so you know the profit target you need to beat.

What's the difference between an SBA 7(a) and 504 loan?

A **7(a) loan** is the most common and flexible, usable for working capital, inventory, or refinancing debt. A **504 loan** provides long-term, fixed-rate financing for major fixed assets like real estate or equipment that promote business growth and job creation.

How much business loan can I afford?

Lenders look at your **cash flow**. Use the optional fields in our calculator. If your new loan payment consumes more than 30-40% of your net monthly income (revenue minus expenses), you may be over-leveraged. Our AI insight will provide a warning in this scenario.
