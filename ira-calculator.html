‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Roth IRA and Traditional IRA?

Traditional IRA contributions may be tax-deductible, earnings grow tax-deferred, and you pay taxes on withdrawals. Roth IRA uses after-tax contributions with tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement.

What are the 2025 IRA contribution limits?

2025 IRA limit is $7,000 for those under 50, and $8,000 for age 50+ (includes $1,000 catch-up). This is a combined limit for all IRAs.

Can I contribute to both Roth and Traditional IRA?

Yes, but combined contributions cannot exceed the annual limit ($7,000 or $8,000 if 50+).

What is a backdoor Roth IRA?

Strategy for high earners: contribute to Traditional IRA (non-deductible) and immediately convert to Roth to access Roth benefits despite income limits.

What is Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)?

Minimum amount required to withdraw from Traditional IRA starting at age 73. Roth IRAs have no RMD requirements during account owner's lifetime.

Can I withdraw from Roth IRA early without penalty?

You can withdraw contributions anytime penalty-free. Earnings have restrictions before 59¬Ω unless you qualify for exceptions like first-time home purchase.

What is the Roth IRA income limit?

2025 phase-out: $150k-$165k (Single), $236k-$246k (MFJ). If income exceeds limits, consider backdoor Roth strategy.

Is Traditional IRA contribution tax deductible?

May be deductible if no workplace plan, or deduction phases out: $79k-$89k (Single), $126k-$146k (MFJ) if workplace plan active.

What is a spousal IRA?

Non-working spouse can have own IRA funded from working spouse's income (filing jointly). Both can contribute up to limits if combined income sufficient.

What is a SEP IRA?

For self-employed and small business owners. Contribute up to 25% of net self-employment income or $70,000 (2025), whichever is less.
